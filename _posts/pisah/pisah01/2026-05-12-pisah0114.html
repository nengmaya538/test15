Albania is a country that packs an extraordinary variety of experiences into a remarkably compact area. In a single week, you can wander through a UNESCO World Heritage stone city, hike in the majestic Albanian Alps, and swim in the turquoise waters of the Ionian Sea. In two weeks, you can do all of that and still have time to explore the vibrant capital, discover ancient Roman ruins, relax on pristine beaches, and indulge in some of the most authentic and delicious cuisine in Europe. The challenge for many travelers is not deciding whether to visit Albania, but figuring out how to structure their limited time to capture the very best of what the country has to offer. The possibilities can feel overwhelming. This comprehensive itinerary planner is designed to solve that challenge. We will provide carefully crafted travel routes for trips of various lengths, from a fast-paced one-week highlights tour to a leisurely two-week grand journey, each optimized for different interests and travel styles.

Whether you are a history buff eager to explore every ancient ruin, a nature lover who wants to hike the high passes and swim in hidden waterfalls, a beach enthusiast seeking the perfect cove, or a foodie on a culinary pilgrimage, this guide has an itinerary designed for you. We understand that every traveler is different. Some want to see as much as possible in a short time. Others want to travel slowly, immersing themselves deeply in a few places. Some are traveling on a tight budget, relying on public transport. Others have the freedom of a rental car. This article provides flexible, practical, and realistic plans that take into account the real travel times, the seasonal considerations, and the logistical nuances of travel in Albania. By the end, you will have a clear, actionable plan for the Albanian adventure of your dreams.

Albania Itinerary Planning Basics

Before diving into specific itineraries, it is helpful to understand the fundamental geography and travel logic of Albania. The country is roughly divided into three main travel regions. The northern region is centered on Shkoder and the Albanian Alps, a world of dramatic mountain peaks, traditional stone villages, and the spectacular Koman Lake ferry. The central region is anchored by Tirana, the vibrant capital, with easy access to the historic city of Berat, the ancient ruins of Durres and Apollonia, and the coastal plain. The southern region is the most touristically developed, encompassing the UNESCO stone city of Gjirokaster, the stunning beaches of the Albanian Riviera, the paradise of Ksamil, and the extraordinary archaeological site of Butrint. Travel between these regions is straightforward, with good roads connecting the major centers. The drive from Shkoder in the far north to Saranda in the far south takes approximately five to six hours on the main highway, a distance that can be covered comfortably in a day. This compact geography is what makes Albania such a satisfying country to explore, allowing you to experience wildly different landscapes and cultures without spending days in transit.

The classic Albanian travel route follows a logical loop or a north-to-south line. Many travelers begin in Tirana, using it as a hub, and then head either north to the mountains or south to the Riviera. A very popular and satisfying two-week itinerary starts in the north with Shkoder and the Koman ferry, continues with the spectacular Valbona to Theth trek, then travels south to Tirana and Berat, continues to Gjirokaster and the Riviera, and finishes with Butrint and Saranda before flying out. This route captures the full diversity of the country. For those with just one week, focusing on either the north or the south is the wisest approach, allowing for a deeper experience rather than a rushed checklist. The choice between relying on public transport or renting a car fundamentally shapes the itinerary. Public transport, using the extensive network of buses and furgons, is cheap and authentic but requires more time and flexibility. A rental car provides maximum freedom and the ability to reach remote areas, but driving in cities like Tirana can be stressful. The itineraries below will include notes on transport options for each route.

Seasonality is a crucial factor. The Albanian Alps hiking season is short and intense, from mid-June to late September. Outside of this window, the Valbona Pass is often snow-covered, and guesthouses in Theth and Valbona close. The Riviera beach season runs from May to October, with July and August being the peak for heat and crowds. The cities and UNESCO sites are year-round destinations, though the cobblestone streets of Berat and Gjirokaster can be treacherously wet in winter. The ideal months for a comprehensive trip covering both mountains and beaches are June and September. These shoulder months offer the perfect balance of warm weather, open trails, warm sea for swimming, and fewer crowds. The itineraries below assume travel during these optimal periods, but alternative suggestions are provided for other seasons. Your personal interests, your travel pace, and the time of year will all shape your perfect Albanian itinerary.

7 Day Classic Albania Highlights

This one-week itinerary is designed for travelers who want to capture the essential highlights of Albania in a limited time. It is a fast-paced but deeply rewarding journey that takes you from the vibrant capital to a UNESCO stone city, the stunning Riviera coast, and an ancient archaeological treasure. The itinerary works best with a rental car, which provides the flexibility needed to cover the distances efficiently. The route begins in Tirana and ends in Saranda, from where you can easily fly out or take a bus back to Tirana. Each day is packed but entirely manageable, and the experiences are curated to provide a rich and varied introduction to the country. This itinerary is perfect for first-time visitors, for those on a short break, or for travelers adding Albania as part of a larger Balkan tour.

Day 1 Tirana: Arrive in Tirana. Spend the afternoon and evening exploring the city on foot. Begin at Skanderbeg Square, visiting the Et'hem Bey Mosque with its beautiful frescoes. Walk through the colorful streets to the trendy Blloku district, where the communist elite once lived in isolation and where today you will find the city's best cafes and restaurants. Visit the House of Leaves museum for a moving insight into the surveillance state of the communist era. Join the evening xhiro, the promenade, and dine at a traditional restaurant serving modern Albanian cuisine. Overnight in Tirana.

Day 2 Berat: Drive from Tirana to Berat, approximately two hours. Arrive in the City of a Thousand Windows. Spend the morning exploring the Mangalem quarter, the most photographed part of the city, with its white Ottoman houses cascading down the hillside. Walk across the beautiful Gorica Bridge for the iconic view back across the river. Visit the Ethnographic Museum, set in a perfectly preserved traditional house. In the late afternoon, drive up to Berat Castle. Walk the ancient walls, visit the Onufri Museum to see the famous icons with their unique red pigment, and watch the sunset from the castle ramparts as the light turns the city below golden. Dine at a restaurant in the Gorica quarter overlooking the illuminated city. Overnight in Berat.

Day 3 Gjirokaster: Depart Berat and drive south, a journey of approximately three and a half hours. The route takes you through the scenic countryside of central Albania. Arrive in Gjirokaster, the Stone City, another UNESCO World Heritage site. The grey slate roofs and imposing stone houses create a dramatic contrast to the white facades of Berat. Walk through the beautifully restored Old Bazaar, watching craftsmen carve wood and weave carpets. Visit the Zekate House, a magnificent fortified Ottoman tower house, to understand the defensive architecture and wealthy domestic life of the city's past. Climb to Gjirokaster Castle in the late afternoon to explore the vast fortress and its museums. The view over the Drino Valley at sunset is unforgettable. Overnight in Gjirokaster.

Day 4 The Blue Eye and Ksamil: A day of natural wonders. Drive from Gjirokaster to the Blue Eye spring, approximately 45 minutes. Arrive early to enjoy the magical setting in the morning peace. Walk through the shaded forest to the brilliant turquoise spring and spend time mesmerized by the deep, swirling water. Continue to Ksamil, a further 30 minutes drive, arriving by early afternoon. Settle into your accommodation and head straight to the beach. The white sand and shallow, crystal-clear water are perfect for a relaxing afternoon. Wade or swim to the small islands just offshore. Enjoy fresh seafood at a beachfront restaurant as the sun sets over the Ionian Sea. Overnight in Ksamil or Saranda.

Day 5 Butrint and the Riviera: Visit Butrint National Park in the morning, arriving as it opens at 8 AM to explore before the heat and the crowds. Spend at least two to three hours wandering through the ancient city, from the Greek theatre to the Byzantine baptistery, the Roman forum, and the Venetian castle on the acropolis. The combination of the ancient ruins and the tranquil forest and lake setting is unforgettable. In the afternoon, drive north along the spectacular Albanian Riviera. Stop at the stunning Porto Palermo castle on its small peninsula. Continue through the dramatic coastal scenery to the village of Dhermi. Enjoy a swim at Drymades Beach and dinner at a cliffside restaurant watching the sunset. Overnight in Dhermi.

Day 6 Llogara Pass and Return to Tirana: A day of spectacular driving. Ascend from the Riviera through the dramatic Llogara Pass, stopping at the top to admire the breathtaking panoramic view over the coast you have just left. Continue through the Llogara National Park forest and descend to the Adriatic coast. Stop at the historic port city of Durres on the way back to Tirana. Visit the magnificent Roman Amphitheater, the largest in the Balkans, and the nearby Byzantine Forum. Enjoy a final seafood lunch on the Volga Promenade before completing the short drive to Tirana. Spend your final evening in the Blloku district, reflecting on your journey over a great meal. Overnight in Tirana.

Day 7 Departure: Enjoy a final traditional Albanian breakfast of byrek and fresh yogurt. Depending on your flight time, squeeze in a visit to the Pazari i Ri market for some delicious souvenirs, local honey, olive oil, mountain tea, or raki. Transfer to Tirana International Airport for your departure.

10 Day Best of Albania Journey

With ten days, you can experience the very best of Albania at a more relaxed and deeply satisfying pace. This itinerary expands on the one-week tour by adding the magnificent Albanian Alps and the spectacular Koman Lake ferry journey in the north, creating a trip that spans the full geographical and cultural diversity of the country. This is the ultimate introduction to Albania, balancing mountains, cities, history, and beaches in a perfectly paced sequence. The route works best with a combination of transport, a rental car is ideal for the flexibility it provides, but the northern mountain section is best done using the established network of guesthouse transfers and local minibuses to avoid the logistical challenge of relocating a car from Theth to Valbona. This itinerary is designed for those who want to see as much as possible without feeling rushed, with time built in for spontaneous discoveries, long meals, and genuine relaxation.

Day 1 Shkoder: Arrive in Tirana and transfer directly to Shkoder, about a two-hour drive to the north. Settle into your accommodation in the historic center. Spend the afternoon exploring the beautiful pedestrian streets, visiting the Marubi National Museum of Photography, a world-class institution, and the atmospheric Site of Witness and Memory museum. Walk to the base of Rozafa Castle as the setting sun lights up the ancient walls. Enjoy a dinner of the famous local dish, tavë krapi, baked lake carp, at a traditional restaurant. Overnight in Shkoder.

Day 2 Koman Lake Ferry to Valbona: An early start for one of the most beautiful boat journeys in Europe. Transfer to the Koman ferry terminal and board the ferry for the spectacular journey through the flooded gorge. The towering cliffs, the turquoise water, and the remote mountain scenery are breathtaking. Arrive in Fierze and transfer by minibus to the Valbona Valley. Settle into your guesthouse and spend the afternoon walking through the valley floor, surrounded by the magnificent peaks. Enjoy a traditional mountain feast prepared by your host family. Overnight in Valbona.

Day 3 Valbona to Theth Hike: The iconic day. Begin the trek over the Valbona Pass early in the morning. The hike takes six to nine hours, climbing through forests, then open alpine meadows, and over the rocky pass at 1,795 meters. The view from the pass, with the Theth Valley unfolding below, is a moment of pure elation. Descend steeply into Theth and arrive at your guesthouse, exhausted and exhilarated. A cold drink and a warm meal have never tasted so good. Arrange for your luggage to be transported by horse so you can hike with just a daypack. Overnight in Theth.

Day 4 Theth Valley: A day to recover and explore the Theth Valley. In the morning, hike to the stunning Blue Eye of Theth, a turquoise pool fed by a powerful waterfall, a magical spot in the forest. In the afternoon, take the shorter, easier walk to the Grunas Waterfall. Visit the historic Kulla e Ngujimit, the Locked-in Tower, to understand the ancient code of the Kanun. Spend the evening relaxing at your guesthouse, sharing stories with other travelers. Overnight in Theth.

Day 5 Tirana: Transfer from Theth back to Shkoder by 4x4 or minibus, then continue to Tirana, a total journey of about four to five hours. Arrive in the capital by mid-afternoon. Settle into your hotel and head out to explore. Walk to Skanderbeg Square, visit the Et'hem Bey Mosque, and then dive into the vibrant Blloku district. The cafés, bars, and restaurants of Blloku are the perfect place to decompress after the mountains. Overnight in Tirana.

Day 6 Berat: A day trip or an overnight to the beautiful city of Berat, a two-hour drive from Tirana. Explore the Mangalem and Gorica quarters, the Ethnographic Museum, and the majestic Berat Castle. The castle is particularly magical in the late afternoon light. Enjoy the local cuisine, including the unique pasha qofte soup. Return to Tirana in the evening or choose to stay overnight in a traditional guesthouse in Berat for a more immersive experience. Overnight in Tirana or Berat.

Day 7 Gjirokaster: Drive from Tirana or Berat to Gjirokaster, approximately three and a half hours from Tirana. Arrive in the Stone City and spend the afternoon exploring its steep, cobbled streets. Visit the imposing castle and the fascinating Zekate House. The view over the Drino Valley at sunset is spectacular. Enjoy a dinner of the local specialty, qifqi rice balls, in a traditional restaurant in the Old Bazaar. Overnight in Gjirokaster.

Day 8 Blue Eye and Ksamil: Visit the magical Blue Eye spring in the early morning, then continue to the coastal paradise of Ksamil. Spend the afternoon swimming in the turquoise water and wading to the islands. Relax on the white sand beach. Enjoy a seafood feast at a beachfront restaurant. Overnight in Ksamil or Saranda.

Day 9 Butrint and the Riviera: Explore the ancient city of Butrint in the morning. Then drive up the spectacular Riviera, stopping at Porto Palermo and the beautiful beaches along the way. Arrive in Dhermi and enjoy the lively beach scene and the stunning sunset. Overnight in Dhermi.

Day 10 Return to Tirana: Drive back to Tirana via the Llogara Pass and Durres. Visit the Roman Amphitheater in Durres if time allows. Arrive in Tirana for your departure flight, or stay an extra night to enjoy the city's nightlife and do some final souvenir shopping.

14 Day Grand Albania Exploration

Two weeks is the ideal amount of time for a comprehensive and deeply satisfying journey through Albania. This grand itinerary takes the ten-day plan and adds the flexibility to travel more slowly, to include additional destinations, and to build in genuine time for relaxation. With two weeks, you can linger in the cafes of Tirana, spend an extra day in the magnificent valleys of the Alps, add the pristine beaches of the southern Riviera, and explore the ancient sites at a contemplative pace. This itinerary is designed for the traveler who wants to truly immerse themselves in the country, not just to see the sights but to feel the rhythm of Albanian life. It follows the same general route as the ten-day itinerary, with a few key additions and a slower, more luxurious pace throughout. The extra days are distributed to allow for deeper exploration and the kind of spontaneous, unstructured time that often yields the most memorable travel experiences.

Days 1 through 10 follow the same routing as the 10-day itinerary above, with the recommended modifications of staying overnight in Berat rather than day-tripping, and taking the full two nights in Theth to properly enjoy the valley. The additional days are woven in as follows:

Day 11 Himara and the Central Riviera: Instead of rushing through the Riviera, dedicate a full day to exploring its heart. Drive from Dhermi to the nearby town of Himara. Explore the atmospheric old town of Kastro on the hill, with its stunning sea views. Spend the afternoon on the beautiful, long Livadhi Beach. Enjoy a long, lazy seafood lunch at a beachside taverna. The pace is relaxed and indulgent. Overnight in Himara.

Day 12 Borsh and Qeparo: Continue exploring the quieter, southern stretch of the Riviera. Visit the vast, unspoiled Borsh Beach, one of the longest in the country. Explore the haunting, abandoned stone village of Upper Qeparo on the hillside. Visit the impressive Borsh Castle, also known as Sopot Fortress. The olive groves of this region produce some of the finest olive oil in Albania. Enjoy a final, quiet evening on the Riviera. Overnight in Borsh or Lukova.

Day 13 Apollonia: On the journey back north, take a detour to the magnificent ancient city of Apollonia. This serene archaeological park, set on a hilltop overlooking the Adriatic plain, is one of the most beautiful classical sites in the country. The elegant facade of the Bouleuterion, the small but excellent museum, and the peaceful atmosphere are a perfect final historical experience. Continue to Tirana in the late afternoon. Overnight in Tirana.

Day 14 Tirana and Departure: Enjoy a final, full day in the capital. Take the Dajti Ekspres cable car for a panoramic view over the city. Visit the Bunk'Art 1 museum for a fascinating and chilling journey into the communist past. Spend the afternoon shopping for souvenirs at the Pazari i Ri market. Enjoy a farewell dinner at one of Tirana's excellent restaurants. Transfer to the airport for your departure.

7 Day Albanian Riviera Beach Escape

This itinerary is designed for those who want to focus purely on the sun, sea, and sand of the stunning Albanian coastline. It is a week of pure relaxation, slow travel, and the simple pleasures of the Mediterranean. The route begins in Saranda, the southern gateway to the Riviera, and slowly moves north, exploring the best beaches, the charming coastal villages, and the hidden coves at a gloriously unhurried pace. A rental car is recommended for maximum flexibility, but the coastal bus service connects the main towns. The itinerary is designed for the summer months and prioritizes time on the beach and long, lazy seafood meals with a view. This is not a trip for rushing. It is a trip for savoring.

Day 1 Saranda and Ksamil: Arrive in Saranda. Settle into your accommodation and head straight to Ksamil, just a 20-minute drive away. Spend the afternoon swimming in the turquoise water, wading to the islands, and relaxing on the white sand beach. Enjoy your first seafood dinner on a terrace overlooking the Ionian Sea. Overnight in Saranda or Ksamil.

Day 2 Butrint and the Islands: A morning visit to the magical ancient city of Butrint, exploring the ruins in the cool early hours. Return to Ksamil for an afternoon of island hopping by pedal boat or kayak. Snorkel in the crystal-clear water around the furthest islands. Overnight in Saranda or Ksamil.

Day 3 Lukova and the Hidden Coves: Drive north to the quieter stretch of coast around the village of Lukova. Discover the secluded coves and the beautiful, peaceful Lukova Beach. Spend the day away from the crowds, enjoying the pristine nature. Overnight near Lukova or Borsh.

Day 4 Borsh and Qeparo: Explore the vast Borsh Beach and the atmospheric abandoned village of Upper Qeparo. Visit the Borsh Waterfall. Spend another day of peaceful beach time. Overnight near Borsh.

Day 5 Porto Palermo and Himara: Stop at the stunning Porto Palermo castle on its small peninsula. Swim in the calm, clear waters of the bay. Continue to Himara and explore the old town of Kastro in the late afternoon. Overnight in Himara.

Day 6 Dhermi and Drymades: Spend the day on the beautiful Drymades Beach. Enjoy the lively but laid-back beach club scene. Watch the sunset from a cliffside bar. Overnight in Dhermi.

Day 7 Llogara and Departure: A final, spectacular drive up the Llogara Pass. Stop at the top for the breathtaking view back over the Riviera. Descend to the Adriatic coast and continue to Tirana airport for your departure, or extend your trip to explore the city.

7 Day Albanian Alps Hiking Adventure

This itinerary is a pure mountain adventure, designed for hikers and nature lovers who want to immerse themselves in the wild beauty of the Accursed Mountains. It focuses entirely on the northern region, combining the beautiful city of Shkoder with the spectacular alpine valleys of Theth and Valbona and the unforgettable Koman Lake ferry. The trek is the centerpiece, but the itinerary also allows for the other wonderful walks each valley offers. This trip requires a good level of fitness and the proper hiking gear. The season is short, from mid-June to late September, and advance booking of the guesthouses is essential.

Day 1 Shkoder: Arrive in Shkoder and spend the day exploring this fascinating city, the cultural heart of the north. Visit Rozafa Castle for the magnificent panorama, and the Marubi Photography Museum. Prepare for the trek, buying any last supplies. Overnight in Shkoder.

Day 2 Koman Ferry to Valbona: The spectacular journey through the mountain gorge. Arrive in the Valbona Valley and spend the afternoon walking the valley floor, breathing the pure mountain air. Feast on traditional guesthouse food. Overnight in Valbona.

Day 3 Valbona to Theth Trek: The big day. The epic hike over the Valbona Pass, a journey through some of the most beautiful mountain scenery in Europe. Arrive in Theth tired, happy, and full of wonder. Overnight in Theth.

Day 4 Theth Valley: A day to rest and explore. Hike to the Blue Eye of Theth, a magical turquoise pool in the forest. Swim in the icy water if you dare. Walk to the Grunas Waterfall. Visit the Kulla e Ngujimit. Enjoy another evening of mountain hospitality. Overnight in Theth.

Day 5 Theth Exploration: A second full day in Theth to explore further afield. Take a longer hike to one of the high alpine pastures or remote peaks surrounding the valley. Enjoy the profound peace and the spectacular views. Overnight in Theth.

Day 6 Return to Shkoder: The scenic and adventurous drive back through the dramatic Theth gorge to Shkoder. Celebrate the completion of the trek with a fine meal in the city. Overnight in Shkoder.

Day 7 Departure: Transfer from Shkoder to Tirana airport, or continue your Albanian adventure southwards.

The journey connects the Illyrian, Greek, Roman, Byzantine, and Ottoman periods, telling the grand narrative of this crossroads of empires. A rental car is essential for this itinerary, providing the freedom to reach the more remote archaeological sites.

Day 1 Tirana: Arrive and visit the National History Museum for a chronological overview of Albania's past. Visit Bunk'Art 2 to understand the communist era.

Day 3 Berat: Explore Mangalem and Gorica quarters. Visit the Ethnographic Museum and the Onufri Museum inside Berat Castle. Overnight in Berat.

Day 4 Bylis: A day trip to the Illyrian city of Bylis, a fascinating and under-visited site with a magnificent theatre and stadium. Return to Berat. Overnight in Berat.

Day 5 Gjirokaster: Travel to the Stone City. Explore the Old Bazaar, the Zekate House, and the vast castle. Overnight in Gjirokaster.

Day 6 Antigonea and Libohove: Visit the ancient Hellenistic city of Antigonea, with its stunning mosaic, in the morning. Explore the quieter stone town of Libohove. Overnight in Gjirokaster.

Day 7 Butrint: Travel to the coast and spend the entire day exploring the extraordinary, layered city of Butrint, a microcosm of Mediterranean history. Overnight in Ksamil.

Day 8 Saranda and Lekuresi: Visit the Lekuresi Castle above Saranda for the panoramic view over the bay and Corfu. Explore the relaxed coastal city. Overnight in Saranda.

Day 9 Return via the Riviera: Drive up the spectacular Riviera, stopping at Porto Palermo and the coastal villages. Overnight in Dhermi.

Day 10 Return to Tirana: Drive back via the Llogara Pass to Tirana for departure.

Transport and Seasonal Considerations

The success of any Albanian itinerary depends on understanding the transport realities and the seasonal variations. Public transport, using buses and furgons, is cheap and extensive, connecting all major towns. It is the most authentic way to travel, but it requires flexibility and patience. Schedules are often loose. For the itineraries that include the Albanian Alps, the combination of public buses to Shkoder, the Koman ferry, and the local minibuses to Valbona and from Theth works very well and is the most popular choice for hikers. For the Riviera, the coastal bus service is frequent in summer but less so in the shoulder seasons. A rental car provides maximum flexibility for the southern and central itineraries and allows you to stop at the countless beautiful viewpoints and hidden places. However, driving in the cities, especially Tirana, can be stressful. If you rent a car, a compact SUV is recommended for the mountain roads. The road to Theth is unpaved and challenging; a high-clearance vehicle is strongly recommended for this access road.

The seasonal variations are dramatic. The summer months of July and August are perfect for beach holidays on the Riviera, but the coastal towns are crowded, and accommodation prices are at their peak. The Albanian Alps are open for hiking, but the valleys are busy. June and September are the golden months for comprehensive trips, offering warm weather, fewer crowds, open mountain trails, and warm sea. Spring, April to May, is glorious for wildflowers, green landscapes, and birdwatching, but the high mountain passes are still snow-covered. Autumn, October, is beautiful with fall colors, and the sea is still swimmable early in the month, though mountain guesthouses may begin to close. Winter is quiet and atmospheric in the cities, but many tourist services in the mountains and on the coast are shut down. Whatever itinerary you choose, always check the current conditions, especially for the Valbona Pass, and book your guesthouses in advance during the peak months. With careful planning and a spirit of flexibility, your Albanian itinerary will unfold into an unforgettable journey through one of Europe's most captivating countries.
